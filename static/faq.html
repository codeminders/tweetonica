I do not use RSS. Can I still use Tweetonica?

First of all if you just do not know what RSS is you should try it out. RSS is very useful for managing information flows from multiple sources. Try a product like Google Reader, NetNewsWire or FeedDemon.

If you do not want to use RSS, you will have to wait a bit to take an advantage of Tweetonica features. Current (beta) version provides information only in RSS format. Next version will have a web interface to read your groups without a RSS reader.

What can I do with Tweetonica?

You can:
  • Create new contact groups
  • Rename existing groups
  • Delete groups
  • Move your contacts from one group to another
  • Download an OPML file and import it into your favorite RSS reader
  • Have links that point to RSS feeds of group's users posts

Twitter provides a RSS feeds. Why not just use them?

Twitter provides single RSS feed for updates from all the people you follow. If you follow more than a handful of people it becomes difficult to manage all updates from all the contacts in single flow. Some updates are more important than others and you would want to be sure you won't miss them among the hudreds of updates you receive daily.

Not only do we help to organize your tweets into groups, but we also make it easier for you to read them. Unlike the twitter RSS feed we try to display the full depth of information embedded in tweets. If somebody tweets an image or video using popular services such as YFrog, YouTube, Twitpic, or Mobypic, we will show you a clickable thumbnails or the embedded video player. All URLs are converted into links. Hash tags, user mentions (e.g. @tweetonica), and stock symbols (e.g. $AAPL) are also converted into links.

How can I move a contact into a group?

Either drag and drop the contact into the desired group at the left or click on the icon next to the user's picture to select a destination group

What happens if I delete a group that contains contacts?

No contacts are lost. All existing contacts are moved to the 'Uncategorized' group

How secure is it?

We take security very seriously. We are using OAuth, so we never ask for your password. You rather enter it at the Twitter web site which gives us authorization to access your account. This authorization could be withdrawn anytime from your preferences at the Twitter.com web site.
